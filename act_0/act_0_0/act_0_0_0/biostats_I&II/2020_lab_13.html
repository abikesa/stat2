HW6 Problem 1

Consider 2x2 table:

2x2 table notation explained

Table entries:

  • \(n_{11}\) - number of patients in treatment group \(A\) who are cases;
  • \(n_{12}\) - number of patients in treatment group \(A\) who are not cases;
  • \(n_{21}\) - number of patients in treatment group \(B\) who are cases;
  • \(n_{22}\) - number of patients in treatment group \(B\) who are not cases.

Column sums:

  • \(n_{+1}\) - number of cases,
  • \(n_{+2}\) - number of controls.

Row sums:

  • \(n_{1+} = n_1\) - number of patients in treatment group \(A\);
  • \(n_{2+} = n_2\) - number of patients in treatment group \(B\);

Total sum:

  • \(n\) - all participants.
2x2 table notation to z-test of proportion differences notation

Proportion of cases among patients in treatment \(A\):

  • \(\hat{p}_1=n_{11}/n_1\)

Proportion of cases among patients in treatment \(B\):

  • \(\hat{p}_2=n_{21}/n_2\)

Pooled estimate of proportion of cases (all cases to all individuals)

  • \(\hat{p}=(n_{11}+n_{21})/(n_{1}+n_{2}) = (\hat{p}_1 n_1 +\hat{p}_2 n_2)/(n_{1}+n_{2})\)

Finally, the square of Z statistic, assuming pooled estimate of proportion of cases, is (lecture 18 slide 12) \[\Big(\frac{\hat{p}_1-\hat{p}_2}{SE_{\hat{p}_1-\hat{p}_2}}\Big)^2=\frac{(\hat{p}_1-\hat{p}_2)^2}{\hat{p}(1-\hat{p})\big(\frac{1}{n_1}+\frac{1}{n_2}\big)}.\].

2x2 table notation to Pearsons \(\chi^2\) test

Pearsons \(\chi^2\) test \(H_0\): There is no relationship between the two variables (here: case/non-case status, treatment group).

  • Compare with z-test \(H_0\): There is no statistical difference between the means (here: proportion of case/non-case status) of the two groups (here: treatment group).

Example for a cell of number of patients in treatment group \(A\) who are cases (upper-left cell):

  • Observed: \(n_{11} = n_1 \hat{p}_1\)
  • Expected under null hypothesis: \(n_1 \hat{p}\)
Derivation

We will show equivalence of

  • The squre of the \(Z\) statistic is (lecture 18 slide 12) \[\Big(\frac{\hat{p}_1-\hat{p}_2}{SE_{\hat{p}_1-\hat{p}_2}}\Big)^2=\frac{(\hat{p}_1-\hat{p}_2)^2}{\hat{p}(1-\hat{p})\big(\frac{1}{n_1}+\frac{1}{n_2}\big)},\] where \(\hat{p}=\frac{n_1\hat{p}_1+n_2\hat{p}_2}{n_1+n_2}\).

The \(\chi^2\) statistic can be rewritten as \[\begin{align*} \chi^2&=\frac{(n_1\hat{p}_1-n_1\hat{p})^2}{n_1\hat{p}}+\frac{(n_1(1-\hat{p}_1)-n_1(1-\hat{p}))^2}{n_1(1-\hat{p})}+\frac{(n_2\hat{p}_2-n_2\hat{p})^2}{n_2\hat{p}}+\frac{(n_2(1-\hat{p}_2)-n_2(1-\hat{p}))^2}{n_2(1-\hat{p})} \\ &= \frac{n_1(\hat{p}_1-\hat{p})^2}{\hat{p}}+\frac{n_1(\hat{p}_1-\hat{p})^2}{1-\hat{p}}+\frac{n_2(\hat{p}_2-\hat{p})^2}{\hat{p}}+\frac{n_2(\hat{p}_2-\hat{p})^2}{1-\hat{p}}\\ &= \frac{n_1(\hat{p}_1-\hat{p})^2+n_2(\hat{p}_2-\hat{p})^2}{\hat{p}(1-\hat{p})} \\ & =\frac{n_1\hat{p}_1^2+n_2\hat{p}_2^2-2(n_1\hat{p}_1+n_2\hat{p}_2)\hat{p}+(n_1+n_2)\hat{p}^2}{\hat{p}(1-\hat{p})}\\ &=\frac{n_1\hat{p}_1^2+n_2\hat{p}_2^2-(n_1+n_2)\hat{p}^2}{\hat{p}(1-\hat{p})}\\ &= \frac{n_1\hat{p}_1^2+n_2\hat{p}_2^2-(n_1\hat{p}_1+n_2\hat{p}_2)^2/(n_1+n_2)}{\hat{p}(1-\hat{p})}\\ &= \frac{\frac{n_1n_2}{n_1+n_2}(\hat{p}_1-\hat{p}_2)^2}{\hat{p}(1-\hat{p})} = \Big(\frac{\hat{p}_1-\hat{p}_2}{SE_{\hat{p}_1-\hat{p}_2}}\Big)^2 \end{align*}\]

Where:

  • Line 1: write out sum of \(\frac{(observed-expected)^2}{expected}\) over 4 cells of 2x2 table,
  • Line 2: take out \(n_1\) or \(n_2\) outside brackets with square, cancel with numerator,
  • Line 3: algebra; put all 4 elements to common denominator, sum and organize numerator
  • Lines 4-5: algebra
  • Line 6: use definition of \(\hat{p}=\frac{n_1\hat{p}_1+n_2\hat{p}_2}{n_1+n_2}\).
